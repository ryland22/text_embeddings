A tow truck driver was killed in a crash while servicing a school bus on eastbound I-94 near Ann Arbor Tuesday morning, according to the Michigan State Police.

The incident happened at about 10:20 a.m. in Pittsfield Township west of U.S. 23, where an SUV carrying five passengers — two adults and three children — crashed into a school bus that was stopped on the side of the road with engine problems, according to a news release from MSP.

The tow truck driver was assisting the bus driver and was reportedly near the rear of the bus when the accident occurred, according to MSP, which said the man was killed as a result of the crash with the SUV.

The state police said there were no passengers on the bus at the time and that the two adults and three children in the SUV were all in critical condition after being transported to area hospitals.

MSP said they are investigating and that there are no further details at this time.
