“ACTION POINT”: Actor-writer-producer Johnny Knoxville (“Jackass”) stars as the crackpot owner of a low-rent, out-of-control amusement park where the rides are designed with minimum safety for maximum fun. Rated R for sexual content, language, drug use, teen drinking and brief graphic nudity.

“ADRIFT”: After two free spirits (Shailene Woodley and Sam Claflin) fall in love, they set out on an ocean journey and land in the midst of one of the most catastrophic hurricanes in history. Based on a true story. Rated PG-13 for injury images, peril, language, drug use, partial nudity and mature themes. Screenings Thursday.

“FIRST REFORMED”: When a parishioner (Amanda Seyfried) asks the minister of a shrinking congregation in upstate New York (Ethan Hawke) to counsel her husband, a radical environmentalist, the clergyman finds himself plunged into his own tormented past. Facing an equally despairing future, he finds redemption in an act of grandiose violence. Rated R for violent images. Screenings Thursday.

“HOW TO TALK TO GIRLS AT PARTIES”: An alien touring the galaxy breaks away from her group and meets two young inhabitants of suburban London in the late 1970s, a time when the city was under the spell of the Sex Pistols and every teen in Britain wanted to be a punk. John Cameron Mitchell (“Hedwig and the Angry Inch”) directs a cast that includes Elle Fanning, Nicole Kidman, Ruth Wilson and Alex Sharp. Rated R for language, sexual content, drug use and nudity.

“UPGRADE”: In the near-future, technology controls nearly all aspects of life. When Grey, a self-identified technophobe, has his world turned upside down, his only hope for revenge is an experimental computer chip implant called Stem. Logan Marshall-Green, Richard Anastasios and Rosco Campbell. Rated R for violence, grisly images and language. Screenings Thursday.
